# Lilac Afterhours Theme Brief

## Theme Name

Lilac Afterhours

## One-Sentence Concept

A late-night lavender theme with velvet purple, blue-black surfaces, soft white text, and small luminous accents.

## Mood Keywords

- Digital lavender
- Velvet night
- Afterhours
- Moody pastel
- Luminous
- Romantic tech
- Soft contrast
- Creator polish

## Ideal Use Cases

- Music and event pages
- Creator portfolios
- Nightlife, beauty, and fashion concepts
- Streaming overlays
- Editorial interfaces
- Personal dashboards with a moody identity
- Launch pages that need softness and atmosphere

## Palette Direction

Build a dark-pastel system around smoky lilac, digital lavender, plum black, violet blue, pearl white, electric mauve, and soft rose.

Rough families:

- Plum black and violet blue for primary surfaces
- Smoky lilac and digital lavender for brand and panels
- Pearl white for high-contrast text
- Electric mauve for active states and small glow moments
- Soft rose for warmth and secondary highlights
- Blue-grey violet for borders and muted text
- Dusty red or wine for danger states

## Accessibility and Contrast Notes

- Lavender on dark can be readable, but muted purple text can fail quickly; test text tokens carefully.
- Keep pearl/off-white available for primary text and important controls.
- Use mauve glow sparingly and never as the only focus indicator.
- Provide darker and lighter variants so components can layer without turning into a purple fog.

## Token Personality Notes

- Brand: digital lavender or smoky lilac, expressive but not childish.
- Surface: plum black, violet blue, and darker mauve-grey layers.
- Accent: electric mauve for links, active states, and listing-preview punch.
- Danger: wine red or dusty crimson with enough separation from rose accents.
- Success: cool mint-teal or soft green that contrasts with purple.
- Warning: muted amber or peach, not too orange.
- Text: pearl white for primary text, pale lilac-grey for muted text, plum black for light surfaces.

## Suggested Preview and Listing Language

Lilac Afterhours is a moody Apocapalette theme pack for music pages, creator portfolios, nightlife brands, and expressive product concepts. It turns digital lavender, plum-black surfaces, pearl text, and electric mauve accents into a complete tokenized UI palette.

## Avoid

- Do not drift into generic purple gradient SaaS.
- Avoid making every surface purple at the same value.
- Avoid pastel text on pastel surfaces.
- Avoid overly cute lavender cues; this should feel late-night and polished.
- Avoid losing functional state contrast for the sake of atmosphere.
